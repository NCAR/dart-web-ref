The Gulf Stream has been studied since the time of Ben Franklin. This current
runs along the United States' Eastern seaboard, along the western edge of the
North Atlantic Ocean. South of Florida, however, it is known as the Gulf of
Mexico Loop Current.

The Loop Current is a highly variable part of the ocean. Its core current
meanders over the span of days to weeks and erratic features break off of it,
known as mesoscale eddies, that pose threats to human infrastructure such as
offshore energy production facilities.

This project, funded by the National Academy of Sciencies, seeks to improve 
predictions of these mesoscale eddies using state-of-the-art models, data 
assimilation and observing systems.

The scientists involved use DART to combine all three of these aspects to
improve understanding of eddy dynamics within the Gulf of Mexico.
